Brand Development Agreement

Flat Fee Engagement

This agreement is between Weeping Willow Whisperer LLC (referred to throughout as "Willow"), a California limited liability company, and [Client Legal Name] (referred to as "Client"), entered into on [Effective Date].

What follows is a working agreement — the shape of how we'll collaborate, what each of us is responsible for, and what happens if the path changes. It is written plainly on purpose. A contract should protect both sides without making either feel smaller.

1. The Work

Willow will provide brand development services for Client, including the following:

  • [Discovery and positioning]
  • [Naming, if applicable]
  • [Visual identity system — logo, typography, color, usage guidelines]
  • [Messaging framework — voice, tone, core language]
  • [Brand guidelines document]
  • [Other deliverables as specified]

Outside this scope: website design and development, ongoing content creation, marketing campaigns, printing and production costs, photography, illustration commissioned from third parties, and any services not explicitly listed above. Additional work can be added through a written amendment to this agreement, quoted separately.

2. Deliverables and Rhythm

The project will move through three phases:

Discovery — understanding Client's business, audience, and positioning. Typically two to three weeks.

Development — creating the core brand system. Typically four to six weeks.

Refinement — revisions, final files, and handover. Typically two to three weeks.

Total engagement: approximately [X] weeks from the deposit date, subject to the feedback windows described in Section 6. Brand work has its own pace; these timeframes are honest estimates, not rigid deadlines.

3. Fee and Payment

The total flat fee for the work described in Section 1 is [$Amount] USD.

Payment structure: 50% deposit ([$Amount]) due upon signing to secure the engagement and Willow's calendar. Work begins once the deposit clears. The remaining 50% ([$Amount]) is due upon delivery of final files.

Accepted methods: bank transfer (ACH or wire), or other methods agreed in writing.

Invoicing: invoices are issued at signing and upon final delivery. Payment is due within seven (7) days of invoice date.

Late payment: payments more than fourteen (14) days past due accrue a late fee of 1.5% per month on the outstanding balance. Willow may pause work on overdue accounts until payment is current.

4. Revisions

Three (3) rounds of revisions are included for each major deliverable. A round is defined as a consolidated set of feedback delivered in a single written response from Client's named point of contact.

Revisions requested after the third round, or outside the original scope, are billed at [$Rate] per hour, quoted in advance and approved in writing before work proceeds.

Feedback that contradicts previously approved directions may be treated as a new round at Willow's discretion.

5. Ownership

All work produced under this agreement — including drafts, concepts, and final deliverables — remains the intellectual property of Willow until final payment is received in full.

Upon receipt of final payment, full ownership of the approved final deliverables transfers to Client. Client may use, modify, and reproduce the work without further permission or additional fees.

Willow retains the right to display the work in portfolio, case studies, and promotional materials unless Client requests otherwise in writing before the project begins. Willow will never disclose confidential business information in such displays.

Preliminary concepts not selected by Client remain Willow's property and may be repurposed for other projects.

Third-party assets (fonts, stock imagery, licensed elements) carry their own licenses, which will be disclosed to Client. Client is responsible for maintaining those licenses after handover.

6. What the Client Brings

For the work to move well, Client agrees to:

  • Provide a single named point of contact with authority to give feedback and approvals
  • Deliver feedback within five (5) business days of receiving each deliverable
  • Provide all necessary brand inputs, references, and materials in a timely manner
  • Respond to questions that require clarification within three (3) business days

If feedback is delayed beyond these windows, the project timeline extends accordingly. Delays of more than thirty (30) days without communication may be treated as a pause, and Willow reserves the right to adjust timelines, reallocate calendar time, or treat prolonged silence as termination under Section 7.

7. If Either of Us Needs to Step Away

Either party may terminate this agreement at any time with written notice.

If Client terminates, the following applies based on the phase reached:

  • During Discovery: deposit is retained in full; no further payment owed
  • During Development: Client pays 75% of the total fee; Willow delivers work completed to date
  • During Refinement: Client pays the full fee; Willow delivers all completed work

If Willow terminates for reasons other than Client breach, Willow will refund any portion of the deposit corresponding to work not yet performed, and deliver all work completed to date.

If Client breaches this agreement — including non-payment, prolonged unresponsiveness, or abusive conduct — Willow may terminate immediately, retain all payments received, and is released from further obligation.

8. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to protect each other's confidential information — including business strategies, financial details, unreleased work, internal processes, and any information marked or reasonably understood as confidential.

This obligation continues beyond the end of this agreement. Neither party will disclose confidential information to third parties without written permission, except where required by law.

9. Limits

Willow's total liability under this agreement — for any claim, regardless of cause — is limited to the total fees paid by Client under this agreement.

Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages, including lost profits, lost business opportunities, or reputational harm.

Willow provides creative services in good faith but does not guarantee specific business outcomes, sales results, or market performance.

10. Governing Law

This agreement is governed by the laws of the State of California.

If a dispute arises, both parties agree to attempt resolution through good-faith mediation before pursuing litigation. Mediation costs will be shared equally. Any litigation will take place in San Diego County, California.

11. General

Entire agreement: this document represents the full agreement between the parties and supersedes any prior discussions or understandings.

Amendments: changes to this agreement must be made in writing and signed by both parties.

Independent contractor: Willow operates as an independent contractor, not an employee, partner, or agent of Client.

Force majeure: neither party is liable for delays caused by circumstances beyond reasonable control — including illness, natural disasters, or other significant disruptions.

Severability: if any clause is found unenforceable, the remaining clauses remain in effect.

Consulting Retainer Agreement

Monthly Advisory Engagement

This agreement is between Weeping Willow Whisperer LLC (referred to throughout as "Willow"), a California limited liability company, and [Client Legal Name] (referred to as "Client"), entered into on [Effective Date].

A retainer is a different kind of agreement than a project. It's not built around a deliverable — it's built around presence. Willow commits reserved time and ongoing thinking; Client gains steady access to that thinking. What follows are the terms that keep the arrangement fair to both sides.

1. The Engagement

Willow will provide ongoing consulting services to Client in the following areas:

  • [Brand strategy and positioning]
  • [Emotional ergonomics and experience design guidance]
  • [Creative direction and editorial review]
  • [Other advisory areas as specified]

The nature of consulting work is conversational and iterative. Willow's role is to think alongside Client, not to produce finished deliverables unless explicitly agreed.

2. Retainer Scope

Client retains Willow for [X] hours per month of advisory time, which may include:

  • Strategy calls and working sessions
  • Written feedback and recommendations
  • Review of Client materials and work-in-progress
  • Asynchronous communication (email, voice notes, documents)
  • Light deliverables under two hours each

Unused hours do not roll over to subsequent months. Reserved time is reserved time; it cannot be banked.

Work exceeding the monthly cap is billed at [$Rate] per hour, quoted and approved in advance. Alternatively, Client may upgrade the retainer tier going forward.

3. Outside the Retainer

The following are not included in retainer hours and require a separate Statement of Work:

  • Full brand development or rebrands
  • Written pieces over 1,500 words
  • Design production (logos, documents, guidelines, visual systems)
  • Strategic planning documents or formal reports
  • Any project requiring more than four hours of focused work

When a request crosses into project territory, Willow will say so and quote the work separately.

4. Fee and Payment

Monthly retainer fee: [$Amount] USD

Payment terms: the full monthly fee is due on the first day of each month, in advance. Work begins for the month only after payment is received.

Accepted methods: bank transfer (ACH or wire), or other methods agreed in writing.

Late payment: if payment is not received within seven (7) days of the due date, Willow may pause all retainer work until payment is current. Payments more than fourteen (14) days past due accrue a late fee of 1.5% per month on the outstanding balance.

5. Term and Renewal

Initial term: [three months] from the Effective Date.

After the initial term, this agreement automatically renews month-to-month unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days before the next billing cycle.

The initial term exists because retainer relationships need time to find their rhythm. Shorter arrangements rarely produce meaningful results for either party.

6. Availability and Response Time

Willow will respond to Client communications within two (2) business days during normal business hours (Monday–Friday, 9am–5pm Pacific Time).

Willow is not available on weekends, holidays, or during announced absences. Urgent matters outside normal hours will be addressed at Willow's discretion.

Willow will provide at least seven (7) days notice before any planned absence of more than three consecutive business days.

7. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to protect each other's confidential information — including business strategies, financial details, internal processes, unreleased work, and any information marked or reasonably understood as confidential.

This obligation continues beyond the end of this agreement.

8. Ownership

Written recommendations, strategic documents, and any materials Willow produces during retainer work become Client's property upon delivery, provided retainer fees are current.

Willow retains ownership of frameworks, methodologies, and internal processes used to produce those recommendations. Client may apply the recommendations freely but does not own Willow's underlying intellectual property or approach.

Willow may reference the working relationship in portfolio and promotional contexts unless Client requests otherwise in writing.

9. Termination

Either party may terminate this agreement:

  • At any time during month-to-month renewal: with thirty (30) days written notice. Fees for the notice period are owed in full.
  • For breach: immediately, with written notice, if the other party materially breaches this agreement and fails to cure within ten (10) business days.
  • For non-payment: Willow may terminate immediately if retainer fees are more than thirty (30) days overdue.

Upon termination, Willow will deliver any work-in-progress completed through the termination date. No refunds are issued for the current month's fee once work has begun.

10. Limits

Willow's total liability under this agreement — for any claim, regardless of cause — is limited to the total retainer fees paid by Client during the three (3) months preceding the claim.

Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages.

Willow provides advisory services in good faith but does not guarantee specific business outcomes or results.

11. Governing Law

This agreement is governed by the laws of the State of California. Disputes will be resolved through good-faith mediation before litigation. Mediation costs are shared equally. Litigation, if necessary, will take place in San Diego County, California.

12. General

Independent contractor: Willow operates as an independent contractor.

Amendments: changes must be made in writing and signed by both parties.

Entire agreement: this document represents the full agreement between the parties.

Severability: if any clause is found unenforceable, the remaining clauses remain in effect.

Writing Commission Agreement

Per-Piece Engagement

This agreement is between Weeping Willow Whisperer LLC (referred to throughout as "Willow"), a California limited liability company, and [Client Legal Name] (referred to as "Client"), entered into on [Effective Date].

Writing is a quiet kind of labor — it requires time to think, time to write, and time to let the draft rest before returning to it. This agreement sets the terms under which Willow will produce commissioned writing for Client, with enough structure to protect both sides and enough room for the work to breathe.

1. The Writing

Willow will produce the following written work for Client:

Project: [Title or working title]

Type: [Essay / report / manifesto / long-form document / brand narrative / other]

Approximate length: [X] words (with a working range of plus or minus 15%)

Purpose and audience: [One to two sentences describing what the piece is for and who will read it]

Voice and tone: [Client voice / Willow voice / blended — specified here]

2. Outside the Scope

The following are not included in this commission and require separate agreement:

  • Additional written pieces beyond the one described above
  • Editing of Client's existing writing
  • Research beyond normal background reading
  • Interview transcription or primary source research
  • Design, layout, or typesetting of the final piece
  • Publication, distribution, or promotion of the work

3. Drafts and Revisions

Willow will deliver the work in the following sequence:

First draft — complete piece, delivered within the timeline described in Section 4. This draft is meant to be reacted to as a whole.

Second draft — revised based on Client's consolidated feedback on the first draft.

Final draft — incorporating refinements from Client's feedback on the second draft.

Three rounds of revisions are included. A round is defined as a consolidated set of editorial feedback delivered in writing from Client's named point of contact.

Additional rounds, or substantive restructuring requested after the second draft, are billed at [$Rate] per hour, quoted and approved in advance.

Feedback must be editorial, not generative. Client can ask Willow to cut, sharpen, restructure, or reconsider. Client cannot use feedback rounds to commission entirely new material or shift the project's premise.

4. Timeline

Estimated delivery schedule from the deposit date:

  • First draft: [X] weeks
  • Second draft: [X] weeks after first-draft feedback received
  • Final draft: [X] weeks after second-draft feedback received

Timelines assume Client provides feedback within the window described in Section 7. Writing has its own pace; these estimates are honest, not rigid.

5. Fee and Payment

Total commission fee: [$Amount] USD

Payment structure: 50% deposit ([$Amount]) due upon signing to secure the work. Remaining 50% ([$Amount]) due upon delivery of the final draft.

Accepted methods: bank transfer (ACH or wire), or other methods agreed in writing.

Late payment: payments more than fourteen (14) days past due accrue a late fee of 1.5% per month on the outstanding balance. Willow may pause work on overdue accounts.

6. Rights and Ownership

Willow retains full ownership of the work until final payment is received.

Upon final payment, Client receives [first publication rights / exclusive rights / non-exclusive rights / full copyright transfer] — specified per commission.

If first publication rights: Client holds exclusive rights to publish the work first. After initial publication, rights revert to Willow, and the work may be republished by Willow with attribution to the original publication.

If exclusive rights: Client holds exclusive publication rights for a period of [X] months/years from final delivery. After that window, rights revert to Willow.

If full copyright transfer: all rights transfer to Client upon final payment. Willow retains the right to reference the work in portfolio contexts only.

Attribution: Willow is credited as author unless the work is explicitly commissioned as ghostwriting, which must be noted in this agreement.

Ghostwriting note: [If applicable] This is a ghostwriting commission. Willow waives public attribution and agrees not to claim authorship publicly. Willow may reference the work privately in portfolio contexts without naming Client.

7. What the Client Brings

For the work to move well, Client agrees to:

  • Provide a single named point of contact
  • Deliver feedback on each draft within seven (7) business days of receipt
  • Provide any necessary background materials, references, or source documents at the start of the project
  • Respond to clarifying questions within three (3) business days

If feedback is delayed beyond these windows, the timeline extends accordingly.

8. Termination and Kill Fee

Either party may terminate this agreement with written notice.

If Client terminates, payment is owed based on the stage reached:

  • Before first draft delivery: deposit is retained; no further payment owed
  • After first draft, before second: Client pays 75% of the total fee; Willow delivers the first draft as-is
  • After second draft: Client pays the full fee; Willow delivers all drafts completed to date

If Willow terminates for reasons other than Client breach, Willow will refund any portion of the deposit corresponding to work not yet performed.

9. Confidentiality

Both parties agree to protect each other's confidential information. Any source materials or background shared by Client for the purpose of the commission will be treated as confidential unless Client specifies otherwise.

10. Limits

Willow's total liability under this agreement is limited to the total fees paid by Client under this agreement. Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages.

Willow warrants that the work will be original and will not knowingly infringe on any third-party rights. Willow does not guarantee publication acceptance, audience response, or commercial outcomes.

11. Governing Law

This agreement is governed by the laws of the State of California. Disputes will be resolved through good-faith mediation before litigation. Litigation, if necessary, will take place in San Diego County, California.

12. General

Independent contractor: Willow operates as an independent contractor.

Amendments: changes must be made in writing and signed by both parties.

Entire agreement: this document represents the full agreement between the parties.

Severability: if any clause is found unenforceable, the remaining clauses remain in effect.
